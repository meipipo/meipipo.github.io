TOMODACHI STEM @ Rice University 2019

February to March 2019
in Houston TX, Philadelphia PA and Washington, D.C.

The TOMODACHI STEM @ Rice University for Female Students is a five-week research internship program for 10 female undergraduates from Japan who are majoring in science & engineering, held at Rice University in Houston, Texas. I was fully funded this trip by TOMODACHI Initiative. I worked on a research project “Predicting Medical Professionals’ Wellbeing Using Wearable and Survey Data” (Poster).

EPATS

April 2017 to September 2018
in New York NY, Cambridge MA, Washington, D.C. and San Francisco CA.

EPATS is a project in which selected Tokyo Tech students arrange short-term overseas study tours themselves and travel abroad in order to develop their global perspectives and abilities to cooperate with people from different cultures and fields. EPATS have been received financial support from Hisao Taki, Founder of GURUNAVI Inc. Whole activities and the study trip were fully funded. See report in Japanese for details.
